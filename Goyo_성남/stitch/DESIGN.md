# Design System Strategy

## Brand Narrative & Visual Identity
This design system expresses a "Modern Editorial" aesthetic—combining the clean clarity of contemporary typography with the approachability of organic shapes. The palette is intentionally restrained, leaning into a high-contrast, minimalist foundation that allows content to remain the primary focus while utilizing soft geometry to create a friendly, accessible user experience.

## Typography
The typographic scale is built on a cohesive, modern pairing:
- **Headlines:** Public Sans. This choice brings a strong, neutral, and highly legible geometric feel to the system, ensuring headers feel fresh and contemporary.
- **Body & Labels:** Inter. A highly legible, neutral sans-serif that ensures clarity and functional efficiency for long-form reading and interface labels.

## Color Palette
The system utilizes a "Neutral" color variant to maintain a clean, professional appearance.
- **Primary (#111111):** A deep, near-black used for primary actions and core text, ensuring maximum contrast.
- **Secondary (#C1C7CE) & Tertiary (#E3E3DC):** Soft, muted grays and off-whites that provide subtle layering and depth without introducing chromatic noise.
- **Neutral (#F9F9F7):** A warm, bone-white base for surfaces and backgrounds, reducing eye strain compared to pure white.

## Shape & Geometry
The visual language has transitioned to a high-softness approach:
- **Corner Radius:** Level 3 (Maximum). All interactive components, such as buttons, input fields, and cards, feature maximum roundedness. This "pill-shaped" geometry softens the high-contrast color palette, making the interface feel modern, inviting, and tactile.

## Spacing & Layout
- **Density:** Level 2 (Normal). The system employs a standard, balanced approach to whitespace. It avoids the claustrophobia of dense layouts while maintaining enough information density to be efficient for functional tools. Elements are given room to breathe, reinforcing the editorial, structured feel of the typography.

## Component Principles
- **Buttons:** Fully rounded (pill) shapes with Primary (#111111) fills and white text for high-visibility CTAs.
- **Surfaces:** Use Tertiary and Neutral values to define hierarchy through subtle value shifts rather than heavy borders.
- **Interactions:** Transitions should feel fluid and organic, mirroring the softness of the corner radii.
